As the time set upon 2015, I took a trip to Mysore > Ooty, which was preceded by my Good Bye to Bangalore. I'm shifitng to Mumbai.

So there I was, sitting on the Ooty Lake in Ooty, mulling over what my resolution for 2016 should be?
(because nice weather and chilled beer generally bring out the thinker in me).

To be honest, I do have a long list of goals this year — 
get in a toned shape,
learn how to shave, (I hate shaving :P)
get rid of these messy & long hairs (My Mothers Dream :P),
write something once a week,
get better at frisbee,
among other important things like taking many of my social projects to its pinnacle and sending my parents on their first trip abroad.

But it came to me that there’s once critical change I must make to my personality, something that has done more harm than good to me in the past. And something that I’d answer if I ever get asked, “What is your greatest weakness?”.

Attachment.

I’ve come to realize, especially over the past 3-4 years, is that I get attached to things too soon, too strongly — to tangible and intangible objects, to people, and to emotions.

2015 has been a roller-coaster ride for me, both personally and professionally. There have been so many crests and troughs, some I had to endure with those closest to me, while the others, all alone. But in most cases, those which involved things that I had gotten attached to have hurt the most.

So 2016 is going to be the year I’ll try out this experiment. I’ll shun all attachment to everything whatsoever.

But how exactly?

That, I need to find an answer soon. It’s easier said than done, and it takes a sage to do it, which I’m aware of. But one small step at a time, and I shall do it. Slowly, but surely.

Oh, and happy new year. May 2016 be your best one ever!
